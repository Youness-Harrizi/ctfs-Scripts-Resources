    timeroasting

     attack technique, which abuses the NTP protocol in order to extract password hashes for computer and trust accounts from a domain controller, which can then be attempted to be cracked offline. It turns out it is not uncommon for such accounts to have bad (default) passwords instead of the frequently rotated random passwords that are normally used, making password cracking possible in those cases.
